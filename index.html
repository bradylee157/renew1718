Frequently Asked Questions

Will Renew continue after our May 20 joint service? And if so, are we going to continue to meet in Renew Groups?

Yes and yes! Renew is going to continue beyond the Gospel Transformation series and we desire that our people continue to meet in Renew Groups. We believe that ministry through these small groups is an effective, sustainable way to learn about Jesus, care for one another, and apply to our lives what we’ve heard on Sundays.

You use the terms small groups and Renew Groups to describe what we’re doing. Are they the same thing?

Yes, one and the same! Renew Groups are what we have decided to call our small group ministry, both in light of our mission of seeking the renewal of Annapolis as we are being renewed by the gospel and as a continuation of the work God has been doing in our midst this past year through Renew.

What happens when the summer starts?

This has been a big year, so whether you are needing a rest or gearing up for more there is a place for you. Your group has the freedom to keep right on going, to merge or multiply, or to take a break until the fall. (If you do take a break, we still encourage you to get together for a meal or an EP event in the meantime!) The Disciple Making Committee is working with each group leader to help make these decisions. We also know that schedules change, so you are free to sign up for a different group. Registration for the summer opens May 20.

What is the curriculum going to look like?

Great question! With over 50 groups and 450 participants, we recognize that answers may look different from group to group and person to person. We have seen tremendous fruit from coordinating the content between sermons and Renew Group discussion, so we will continue to offer that option. Other options will be available based on the needs and focus of each group. The fundamental basis for selecting curriculum is that it be gospel centered and mission focused.

What if I am not in a Renew Group?

The invitation is always open for you to enter more deeply into the EP family by considering involvement in a Renew Group. Summer registration for groups opens May 20, and we invite you to use this as an opportunity to connect more fully to the local body of Christ. It is in our Renew Groups that we learn and grow, care for each other, fellowship together, and seek to welcome others into life at EP.

Could you remind me, just what exactly is a Renew Group?

Renew Groups provide a gospel-renewal experience in the context of outward-focused communities that encourage and equip us to love God, love people, and love Annapolis. A Renew Group is a safe place where you will learn to confess, love, and serve Christ together; study Scripture together; grow in your experience of God’s grace; and increase your passion for making disciples beyond the walls of our church.

Who should be involved in a Renew Group?

Anyone and everyone! If you attend Worship here at EP on a regular basis, have your children involved in our Student or Children’s Ministry, or have even completed our Membership class, we invite you to enter more deeply into the EP family by stepping into a Renew Group. These small, intentional communities are one of the best ways to mature in your relationship with God and others.

Evangelical Presbyterian Church

Over the past five decades, EP Church has helped thousands of people in Annapolis and the surrounding area find meaning, purpose, and belonging. With Jesus as Lord, our desire is to be a group of believers renewed by the Gospel who in turn are seeking the renewal of our city and those who live here. The Gospel has changed who we are, and we hope to live out that good news so that others may fall in love with our Savior.
